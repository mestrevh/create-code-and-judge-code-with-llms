Aldous Leonard Huxley (26 July 1894 | 22 November 1963) was an English
 writer and one of the most prominent members of the famous Huxley family.
 Best known for his novels including Brave New World and a wide-ranging
 output of essays, Huxley also edited the magazine Oxford Poetry, and
 published short stories, poetry, travel writing, and film stories and
 scripts. Huxley spent the later part of his life in the United States,
 living in Los Angeles from 1937 until his death.
